Difference Between Computer Architecture And Computer ... Computer organization refers to the physical structure and interconnection of the components within a computer system, encompassing how hardware elements work together to execute instructions. It's the realization of the architecture, focusing on operational attributes like control signals, hardware details, and peripherals. Here's a more detailed breakdown: Interconnection of Components: Computer organization deals with how the CPU, memory, input/output devices, and other hardware components are physically connected and communicate. Hardware Implementation: It focuses on the practical implementation of the architectural specifications, including the design of circuits, buses, and other hardware elements. Operational Attributes: Computer organization encompasses the operational aspects of the system, such as how data is moved, processed, and stored. Distinction from Architecture: While computer architecture defines the system's overall structure and functionality as seen by the software, computer organization focuses on the hardware realization and implementation details. Examples: CPU organization: The arrangement of registers, arithmetic logic unit (ALU), and control unit within the CPU. Memory organization: How memory is structured, accessed, and managed. Input/Output organization: How input and output devices are connected and controlled.
